Our Story

Meet Margaux

Some people doom-scroll. I decoupage. What started as one rescued tray, a jellyfish print and a very steady hand has turned into TrayChic — a small-batch studio practice with a serious gold-leaf habit.

Every tray begins life as honest wood. I hunt down the prints — Haeckel's jellyfish, prowling tigers, coiled snakes, moonlit pines — tear, layer and gild by hand, then flood the lot with resin until it shines like glass. It's slow. It's fiddly. It's completely worth it.

They're built to be used: breakfasts, negronis, keys, jewellery, remote controls that finally have a home. Beauty with utility — just louder.

The Craft

How a TrayChic tray happens

01

The hunt

Vintage natural-history plates, cartoons and skylines — sourced, cut and composed until the tray tells a story.

02

The layers

Hand-torn papers, painted borders and crackle finishes, built up layer by patient layer.

03

The gold

Real gold leaf, laid by hand. It flashes when the light moves. That's the point.

04

The gloss

Deep resin pour for a glass-like, wipe-clean finish. Gorgeous AND dishwasher-adjacent (a damp cloth, please).
